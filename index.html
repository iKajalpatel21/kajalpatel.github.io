About

Kajal Patel is a Data Engineer and Research Assistant III at The George Washington University School of Medicine and Health Sciences in Washington, D.C. With over four years of experience, she specializes in real‑time streaming, scalable ETL pipelines and data governance. Currently pursuing her Master’s degree in Computer Science at GWU, Kajal processes more than one terabyte of data daily and maintains 99 percent system uptime.

Her technical toolkit includes Python, SQL, Apache Spark, Kafka, AWS (S3, EMR, Redshift), Databricks and real‑time streaming frameworks. She is passionate about building fault‑tolerant data solutions that drive business intelligence and decision‑making.

Technical Projects

Real‑Time Stock Market Data Pipeline

This end‑to‑end pipeline ingests live stock data from the Finnhub API, streams it through Apache Kafka, processes it with Spark, and stores results in Google BigQuery. The project also includes a Next.js dashboard and Tableau visualizations and is deployed with Docker and Kubernetes.

NYISO Electricity Consumption & Pricing Insights

Using historical data from the New York Independent System Operator, this project analyzes locational marginal prices, grid congestion and demand to forecast price changes, predict demand and detect anomalies. The study spans data from 2001–2023 and leverages Python, PySpark, Scikit‑learn and Matplotlib.

View on GitHub

SEC Corporate Filings Project

This analysis of U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission financial statement data benchmarks companies, clusters them by financial health, detects potential fraud, and maps executive networks using Neo4j and GraphRAG. Tools used include Pandas, PySpark, graph analytics and custom visualizations.

View on GitHub

NYC Taxi Trip Insights

This project explores millions of New York City taxi rides to uncover demand patterns, estimate trip times, analyze payment preferences and build fare prediction models. It uses Python, Pandas, PySpark, Scikit‑learn and Seaborn on large Parquet datasets.
